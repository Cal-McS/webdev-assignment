Places To Visit

The Bo'ness Hippodrome

The Bo'ness hippodrome is a cinema located in the centre of the town. It was created by Louis Dickson and designed by local architect Matthew Steele and was first opened on 11 March 1912. It is the oldest purpose-built picture house in Scotland. The building is owned by the Scottish Historic Buildings Trust. In 2009 the hippodrome was re-opened with updated 21st century technology. The hippodrome hosts an annual silent film festival, Scotland's only festival of silent film featuring live music.

Bo'ness & Kinneil Railway

The Bo'ness & Kinneil railway is a heritage railway located on the shoreline of Bo'ness. A mixture of steam trains and heritage diesel engines are operated on the line. The route taken by the railway is between Bo'ness and Manuel Junction via Kinneil and Birkhill. The railway used to be connected to the main train network, however, it is now disconnected and serves as a tourist attraction. The Bo'ness railway also features the Museum of Scottish Railways.

Bo'ness Town Hall & Fair

The Bo'ness town hall is located in the centre of Bo'ness. It formerly served as the headquarters of the burgh council for much of the 20th century. The town hall serves as the location for the crowning of the Bo'ness Children's Fair Festival. The Fair started in 1897 and is still the most important event of the year in Bo'ness. The Fair was originally started as a celebration by miners following an Act whiched freed the miners from virtual slavery, however, it is now a children's festival.

Kinneil Estate

Kinneil Estate is a large public park and nature reserve located on the west outskirts of Bo'ness. The main feature of Kinneil Estate is Kinneil House which was built in 1677. Kinneil Estate also has a ruined Roman fortlet that was part of the Antonine Wall as well as James Watt's cottage behind Kinneil House where he conducted experiments during the development of the steam engine. There is also a museum on the road next to Kinneil House that showcases the history of the area.

The Bo'ness Hill Climb

The Bo'ness Hill Climb is an annual motorsport event that takes place in Kinneil Estate. The event first started in 1932 and became the first ever round of the British Hill Climb Championship in 1947. One notable winner of the event was multiple time Formula One champion Jim Clark. The hill climb was forced to stop in 1966 but in 2008 it was brought back and is now a revival event where people with a passion for motorsport showcase their revived classic and historic cars.
